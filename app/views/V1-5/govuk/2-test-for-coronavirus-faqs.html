What is a coronavirus test?

The new coronavirus is called SARS-CoV-2. In some people it leads to the disease COVID-19.

Who can be tested?

Key workers who are self-isolating can be tested:

  • if they currently have coronavirus symptoms
  • if one or more other people in theri home have symptoms, but they are not displaying symptoms
  • ccc

The follow individuals are not able to be tested at this time:

  • Key workers in households where no one has symptoms
  • Households where there a no keyworkers
  • Under 18's
  • Some remote geographic locations (e.g. Highlands and Islands)

What do the results mean?

If your test result is negative, it means:

  • you don't currently have coronavirus
  • you can safely return to work, providing anyone you live with also tests negative

If your test positive, or someone you live with tests positive, it means:

  • the person who tested postive has coronavirus
  • key workers in the household cannot return to work.
  • Everyone in the household needs to continue to self-isolate.
  • Read NHS advice about how long to self isolate.
